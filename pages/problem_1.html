Problem Statement

In today's academic environment, high school students are constantly making decisions about what classes to take, what clubs to join, and how to prepare for life after graduation. Even motivated students often struggle to connect their choices to a larger sense of purpose. They are surrounded by opportunities, but don’t always see how those fit together or where they might lead. Many stick with what feels familiar, not because they aren’t curious but because there isn’t a clear path forward.

Who We Spoke To (Stakeholders)

We know that students aren’t navigating high school alone. Their choices and growth are shaped by a larger network of people each with different goals and perspectives. To design for this complex system, we spoke with:
Student
I want to try new things but don’t know where to start.

Students today are motivated but often feel overwhelmed by choices. They want personalized guidance that connects their interests to concrete opportunities. Many feel stuck in rigid academic tracks that don't reflect their true passions or potential career paths.

Teacher
Knowing students’ interests lets me shape better lessons.

Teachers want to create engaging, relevant curriculum but struggle with large class sizes and limited insight into individual student motivations. They need better tools to understand and connect with each student's unique learning style and interests.

Parent
If I knew what motivated my child, I could support them better.

Parents want to support their children's growth but often feel disconnected from their school experience. They struggle to balance providing guidance while allowing independence, and need better visibility into their child's interests and progress.

Counselor
With limited time per student, it’s hard to build deep connections.

School counselors are overwhelmed with caseloads of 200+ students each. They need efficient ways to track student interests, identify at-risk students, and provide meaningful guidance that goes beyond just course scheduling and college applications.

Why This Matters

Students hesitate to take risks because they don’t see the value and the system around them doesn’t show it either. Exploration and curiosity can feel confusing or risky. Without the right support or tools, it’s easier to follow the expected path than to imagine new ones.

Stack shifts that dynamic. By making students’ progress visible, their options clear, and their decisions feel supported. It helps them explore with purpose instead of pressure. This isn’t just about planning for the future, it’s about giving students more agency in the present.

Our Solution: Stack

Stack makes progress visible, paths clearer, and choices feel supported. It helps students reflect, explore, and act—on their terms.
