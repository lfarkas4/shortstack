Key Insights

Guided
Reflection
Students need structured ways to reflect on their experiences to build self-awareness, identify patterns, and make more confident decisions.
"My goal is to give them goal clarity... to understand what they want and why" — Teacher (Campaign Craft, Week 3)
Dynamic
Visualization
Visualizing growth and connections over time helps students see value in their learning journey, even through pivots or failure.
"Helps reduce stress and anxiety by showing concrete steps to get to where they want to go." — English Teacher (Bubble Builder, Week 1)
Low-Risk
Exploration
Personalized recommendations reduce decision fatigue and help students discover meaningful opportunities without the stress of seeking them out.
"It felt safe for my kid to try things out — like there wasn't a wrong answer." — Parent, Poster Prototype, Week 2

What We Learned From Each Prototype

1
High school stakeholders value guidance over independence
2
Students value reflection but they are not always sure how to go about articulating it
3
A visual representation of past experiences can aid in finding direction
4
Reframing skills from the lens of hobbies and personality traits can introduce students to more opportunities
5
Onboarding needs to be clear to translate outcomes from actions
6
When in an environment in which peers can see posts, motivations become impressing others, inhibiting self-reflection
7
Showing action outcome upfront encourages participation
8
No matter how great the product is, the user interface needs to be clear and intuitive for students to use
9
Community and shared vulnerability inspire empathy
10
Parents find proof of learning to be important in validating alternative learning methods
11
Giving students guidance — not the most crucial necessity for decision making for students is their own intrinsic motivation
12
Each stakeholder has a unique and important role in student development
